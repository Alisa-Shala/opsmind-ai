# SLA Breach Policy

SLA means Service Level Agreement. In invoice operations, SLA represents the expected time allowed for invoice review and approval.

In OpsMind AI, the expected approval threshold is 10 days. If approval_days is greater than 10, the invoice is considered SLA breached.

SLA breach does not always mean fraud. It means the internal process is delayed. Possible causes include:
- Missing purchase order.
- Duplicate invoice investigation.
- Delayed manager approval.
- Delayed confirmation from the department.
- Vendor documentation issues.
- Finance or Accounts Payable backlog.

SLA-breached invoices should be monitored because they can delay payment, create vendor dissatisfaction, and increase operational risk.
