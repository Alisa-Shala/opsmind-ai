# Missing Purchase Order Policy

A Purchase Order is an internal authorization document created by the company before a purchase is made. It confirms that the company approved the product or service before the vendor submitted the invoice.

A missing purchase order increases risk because Accounts Payable may not know whether the purchase was authorized.

If an invoice is missing a purchase order:
- The invoice should not be auto-approved.
- The case should be routed to Procurement.
- The vendor or internal requester should provide the correct PO reference.
- Payment should be paused until validation is complete.

Missing PO cases are important because they indicate missing financial control.
