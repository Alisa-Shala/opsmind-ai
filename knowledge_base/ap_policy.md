# Accounts Payable Invoice Processing Policy

Accounts Payable is responsible for reviewing invoices before payment approval. Each invoice should be checked for vendor details, invoice amount, purchase order reference, duplicate invoice risk, approval timeline, and payment terms.

A standard invoice can move forward when:
- The vendor is recognized.
- The invoice has a valid purchase order.
- The invoice is not marked as a duplicate.
- The amount is within the expected review threshold.
- The invoice approval is within the SLA timeline.
- The goods or services have been confirmed.

Invoices requiring additional review should be routed to the appropriate team:
- Missing purchase order cases should be routed to Procurement.
- Duplicate invoice cases should be routed to Accounts Payable Analyst.
- SLA breaches should be escalated to Operations Manager.
- High-value or high-risk invoices should be reviewed by AP Manager or Risk/Compliance.
